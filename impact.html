Real-Life Impact & Inspiring Stories

Solar ovens do more than cook food — they transform lives, protect the environment, and build resilience in communities around the world. Here's how they're making a real difference.

Key Benefits & Environmental Impact

  • Healthier lives: Reduces indoor air pollution from open fires, which kills over 1,250 children under age 5 daily (source: Solar Cookers International).
  • Protects forests: One solar cooker can save up to 1 ton of wood per year, helping prevent deforestation in fuel-scarce regions.
  • Climate action: Millions of solar cookers already prevent millions of tons of CO₂ emissions annually — equivalent to taking hundreds of thousands of cars off the road.
  • Saves money & time: Families spend less on fuel (sometimes $90+ per year per cooker) and women & children gain hours for education, work, or safety instead of gathering firewood.
  • Safer cooking: Reduces risks of violence during firewood collection and burns from open flames.

Success Story: Kakuma Refugee Camp, Kenya

In the Kakuma Refugee Camp and Kalobeyei Settlement (home to ~298,000 refugees), Solar Cookers International (SCI) has distributed hundreds of sturdy solar cookers since 2018. These cookers last 15+ years and serve families of 6–10 people.

“It [the solar cooker] has been a lifesaver. I could not afford fuel or barter food for fuel, and my only choice was to risk being attacked while collecting firewood.”

— A woman from Kakuma Refugee Camp
  • 400+ solar cookers distributed → impacted 3,120+ refugees directly
  • Over 42 million solar-cooked meals served
  • ~8,940 metric tons of CO₂ avoided over the cookers' lifetimes
  • 93% of participants report using less fuel and saving money
  • Women have become solar cooking champions, leading training and follow-up in their communities

Global Reach & Bigger Picture

More than 4 million solar cookers have been identified worldwide, directly improving life for over 14 million people. If just 25% of meals were solar-cooked in fuel-reliant households, it could prevent 170 million tons of CO₂ emissions annually and save billions in health & environmental costs.

From refugee camps in Kenya to rural communities in Chad, Mexico, and beyond — solar cooking empowers people, saves trees, reduces emissions, and creates safer, healthier futures.
